A Shocking Knife Attack on a UK Train — Terrorism Ruled Out

On the evening of Saturday, 1 November 2025, a violent stabbing took place aboard a train travelling from Doncaster to London. The train made an emergency stop at Huntingdon, Cambridgeshire, where police and medics rushed to the scene. The incident left multiple passengers injured and raised fears of a possible terror attack, which authorities have since ruled out.

Summary: A major knife attack on a UK inter-city train has left 11 people injured, two critically. Two British men have been arrested on suspicion of attempted murder. Investigators confirm the attack was not terrorism-related.

What Happened

Around 7:42 p.m., as the train headed toward London’s King’s Cross station, witnesses described a man running through the carriages with a large knife, attacking passengers. One witness told reporters: “I put my hand on this chair … and then I look at my hand, and it’s covered in blood. And then I look at the chair, and there’s blood all over the chair.”

In total, 11 people were taken to hospital, with two in life-threatening condition.

The Arrest and Investigation

Armed police responded within minutes after the train stopped at Huntingdon. Two men, both British nationals aged 32 and 35, were arrested on suspicion of attempted murder. Officers declared a major incident and activated “Plato,” the UK’s protocol for suspected marauding terror attacks.

Terrorism Ruled Out

By Sunday morning, investigators announced that there was no evidence of terrorism. The motive remains under investigation, but officials called it an isolated incident. The UK’s national terror threat level has not changed.

Official Reaction

King Charles III and the Queen said they were “truly appalled and shocked” by the “dreadful knife attack,” expressing deep sympathy for the victims and gratitude to emergency services. The Prime Minister described the attack as “appalling” and “deeply concerning,” while the Home Secretary praised the bravery of staff and passengers and confirmed that the case is not being treated as terrorism.

Why This Matters

Train travel is central to daily life in the UK, and an attack on a busy inter-city service has shaken public confidence. Initial fears of terrorism caused nationwide concern, but swift police intervention prevented further harm. The attack also highlights a broader issue: knife crime levels in England and Wales remain high compared to previous years.

What We Still Don’t Know

The full motive behind the attack remains unclear. Investigators are still determining whether it was pre-planned or spontaneous, and whether others may have been involved. The identities of the victims and suspects have not been fully released.

What Is Being Done

Police from the British Transport Police and Cambridgeshire Constabulary are working with national counter-terrorism units. A high-visibility police presence has been deployed across key train routes to reassure the public. London North Eastern Railway (LNER) has warned of temporary disruptions on the Doncaster–London line as the investigation continues.

What Passengers Can Do

  • Stay alert and aware of your surroundings while travelling.
  • Report suspicious behaviour to train staff or police immediately.
  • Follow official instructions in emergencies and avoid speculation online.
  • Rely on updates from verified news sources and authorities.

In Summary

A violent knife attack struck a passenger train in Cambridgeshire on 1 November 2025, injuring at least 11 people. Two men have been arrested for attempted murder. Terrorism has been ruled out, but the motive is still being investigated. The King, Prime Minister, and other officials condemned the attack, while authorities strengthen safety measures on UK railways.
